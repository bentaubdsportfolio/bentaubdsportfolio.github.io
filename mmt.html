Multimedia Thinking

This section is for various multimedia assignments done for my Multimedia Thinking course.
For this course we were tasked with picking an issue to address, so the issue I had chosen
was the over-simplification of fighting games.

Photo Assignment

With simplifying of fighting games, the game also changes making it difficult for pro players to adapt, things that were ingrained into their minds as to how to approach a fighting game, now are completely different or in some cases outright unnecessary, giving these players with years of experience the appearance of being bad. Even for Daigo Umehara, a long time pro in the fighting game community, also seen as the first to be that, was known as the best, no one could beat him, and even being known for saving fighting games as we know it. With this watering down of fighting games even he was referred to as a bad player, being ridiculed by many, despite all the accomplishments he has had in the past, with very few being able to approach his success.

The picture I used was a headshot of Daigo Umehara, a pro fighting game player. With the text, I split it up between positive statements, and negative statements that people have used in reference to him. It is then finished off with a tag in the center, labeling him as a “Pro.” With the composition, I had the positive statements placed above him near his head, and had him look down on the negative statements, giving it more of a somber tone.

Comic Assignment

In my comic I used both screens from a game, as well as pictures taken from actual big tournaments. To have the images, which are mixed with both real life and non-real life, match better together on the paper I used a sketch filter so they would appear more similarly. Finally for the text, I repeated the phrase “I can win” over and over, however I left who is saying that and the connotation in which it is being used purposely ambiguous.

Watering down the elements that make anything difficult can leave the pros worried for multiple reasons. Even newcomers who do not have nearly as much experience can still leave the pros worried with if they can win, which is why I left it ambiguous as to who was saying “I can win” in the comic. Also having the way you do things change so you are working harder than necessary can make it harder for the pros to succeed, so even the pros have to worry about whether they can win or lose to people who are just starting out.

Sound Assignment

In my sound, I took the sound of gameplay from a fighting game and put it over 2 other sounds: A cheering crowd and a booing crowd. With that was also overlaid 2 sets of button pressing sounds, one of mashing while the other is in a more rhythmic pattern. Neither of these sets of sounds are ever directed at a specific player of the game.

For my sound, I purpose kept the recipient of the booing and who was mashing the buttons and who was pressing them rhythmically anonymous. By watering down how fighting games are played, the line between those who are novices and those who are experts are skewed. It will become harder and harder to determine this if fighting games are continued to be oversimplified.

Video Assignment

In my video I took one of the most, if not the most well known, influential moments from competitive fighting games and added some effects to it to make it look as though the video is deteriorating as the video progresses. Some of the effects used were blurring, noise, desaturation, reverb, etc. The idea behind this was to make it look like the video was becoming lost to time.

The reason why this specific clip was so well known and loved is because on top of being exciting and interesting to watch, even those that are not familiar with the game can tell that what is going on is something that was difficult to do, it took skill. Lowering the skill ceiling of fighting games will make it so exciting moments like this won’t happen because there won’t be anything particularly hard to do that would get an audience hyped up and excited. These types of moments will be lost.
